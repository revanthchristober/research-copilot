- Short. ~150 words max. The demo is the artifact, this is the framing.
- Specific > generic. Name real projects, real numbers, real choices.
- Show you read the JD. Reference Great Question's actual problems.
- Don't list skills. Tell a story.

## Fill-in worksheet — answer these and I'll draft the final version

1. **Who are you in one line?**
   _e.g. "Final-year CS undergrad in Chennai," "self-taught engineer building AI tools for a year," "bootcamp grad shipping side projects on weekends"_

2. **What's the most impressive AI thing you've built?**
   Include: what it does, what stack, any concrete outcome (users, GitHub stars, accuracy %, anything measurable). One sentence.

3. **What about Great Question specifically pulls you in?**
   Not "I love AI" — something specific. Examples:
   - "The MCP angle — most companies talk about agents, you're shipping tool servers"
   - "Customer research feels like the highest-leverage domain for RAG — you can verify every claim with a quote"
   - "PJ's background shipping Rails for a decade + AI is the exact combo I want to learn from"

4. **One thing you'd push back on or improve in their product**
   (Optional but high-signal — shows you've actually used / studied it)
